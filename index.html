By Oli Sorenson


The text below represents the initial phase in developing the CALQ funded project entitled The Metamonument. Once peer-reviewed, this essay will be integrated into a video animation, and accompanied by a host of related artworks, created as 3D prints, NFTs, self-adhesive vinyl, textile, and other expressive forms. The latter will be assembled into a larger installation, shown at Elektra gallery in Montreal on January 2026, with the aim to erect a monument that informs our present times.

If a monument was once considered a physical manifestation and focal point for established or traditional communities, the creation of a similar structure dedicated to current technological, cultural and geopolitical transformations, seems continually challenged by escalating climate change, economic tensions, armed conflicts and other disruptive events, which taken altogether, threaten to ruin our existing civilization(s).

Yet rather than come to nihilist conclusions, as keys thinkers have done in previous decades, from Francis Fukuyama’s End of History 1 to Mark Fisher’s Hauntology, 2 the following sections imagine the rise of a monument that might cluster together today’s many crises within deeper patterns; assemble them in a coherent field that some have called the metacrisis. 3 To this end, the metamonument is submitted here as an open, distributed yet somewhat incomplete structure, assisting the viewer to navigate the metacrisis mainly via Hito Steyerl’s concept of poor image and Timothy Morton’s hyperobject.

1 Fukuyama, Francis. 1992. The End of History and the Last Man. First Free Press trade paperback edition. New York, NY: Free Press.

2 Fisher, Mark. 2014. Ghosts of My Life. Writings on Depression, Hauntology and Lost Futures. Zero Books: London.

3 The term metacrisis is still in progress, few publications have exhaustively been dedicated to this phenomenon. However, many authors already address specific aspects of the metacrisis, which have been compiled here: https://metacrisis.org/META-CRISIS/01.+%F0%9F%93%9D+Resources/Meta-Crisis+Books+%26+Papers
